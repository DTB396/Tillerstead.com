Tillerstead’s Build Phase: a step-by-step guide to code-compliant bathroom and shower projects in New Jersey, following TCNA, ANSI, and state standards.

On this page

Welcome to the official build documentation for Tillerstead.com. This page outlines our site generation, deployment, and quality control workflows. All steps comply with TCNA 2024 and New Jersey HIC standards.

The Build Phase

The Build Phase is Tillerstead’s standards-forward guide to bathroom and shower projects in New Jersey.
It explains the sequence, the verification points, and the common failure modes—so expectations are clear before anything is sealed behind tile.

This is not a DIY manual. It’s an education and alignment resource for homeowners, designers, and contractors who want a durable, compliant result.

References include:

  • TCNA Handbook methods
  • ANSI tile standards
  • ASTM testing guidance
  • New Jersey Uniform Construction Code (UCC)

Why These Guides Exist

Most bathroom failures don’t start with tile—they start earlier: at framing, waterproofing, slope, drainage, or skipped verification steps that are later hidden by finished surfaces.

These guides help you:

  • Set clear expectations before construction begins
  • Reduce surprises during the build
  • Recognize quality workmanship
  • Understand why each phase exists—not just what it is

What We Build

  • Code-aware bathroom and shower remodels planned around permits, inspections, and verifiable system steps
  • Wet-area assemblies built as systems (structure → slope/drain → waterproofing → tile)
  • Clear documentation habits (photos at key phases; verification before concealment)

What We Don’t Do

  • “Tile-over-problems” work that hides substrate, slope, or waterproofing issues
  • Mixed-system waterproofing assemblies without manufacturer compatibility or engineering rationale
  • Rushed sequencing that skips verification steps (especially before finishes conceal the work)

The Construction Sequence: 8 Phases

Each phase builds on the one before it.
For the full picture, read in order. If you’re researching a specific issue, you can jump directly to the relevant phase.

  1. Codes, Permits & Inspections:
    Understanding New Jersey’s construction code, permit requirements, and inspection sequence.

  2. Shower Pans, Slopes & Drains:
    How water is directed safely to the drain before tile is installed.

  3. Waterproofing Systems:
    Surface-applied vs. traditional systems, continuity, and compatibility.

  4. Curbs & Curbless Showers:
    Containing water at the shower entry, including curbless designs permitted in NJ.

  5. Framing for Benches, Seats & Niches:
    Structural planning that must happen before waterproofing.

  6. Tile Installation Standards:
    How tile is bonded to the system and why preparation matters more than tile choice.

  7. Flood Testing & Verification:
    The final opportunity to verify waterproofing before finishes conceal the work.

  8. Common Failures & Red Flags:
    Where failures originate, warning signs to watch for, and how proper sequencing prevents them.
