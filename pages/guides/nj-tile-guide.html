1 TCNA & ANSI Standards

What is TCNA?

Tile Council of North America provides installation method standards.

  • TCNA Handbook: Installation methods
  • Method EJ171: Standard shower installation
  • Method B421: Waterproof membranes

Key ANSI Standards

  • A108.10: Tile installation specifications
  • A118.10: Waterproofing membrane requirements
  • A118.15: Large format tile adhesive

2 Vetting NJ Contractors

NJ HIC License Required

Work over $500 requires active NJ HIC license. Verify at nj.gov/consumeraffairs/hic

10 Essential Questions

  1. What is your NJ HIC license number?
  2. Do you follow TCNA installation methods?
  3. What waterproofing system will you use?
  4. Do you warranty your work? For how long?
  5. Will you provide a written contract?
  6. Can you provide recent project references?
  7. What is your substrate inspection process?
  8. Modified or unmodified thin-set?
  9. How do you handle change orders?
  10. What is your cleanup process?

4 Material Selection

Porcelain Tile

Pros: Extremely durable, low water absorption, frost-resistant
Best for: Floors, wet areas, high-traffic zones

Ceramic Tile

Pros: Affordable, easier to work with, wide variety
Best for: Walls, backsplashes, light-traffic floors

Large Format Tile (LFT)

LFT Requirements: ANSI A118.15 adhesive required, extremely flat substrate (1/8" in 10 feet max), back-buttering for full coverage, experienced installer necessary.

6 Your Legal Rights in NJ

Contractor Requirements

  • Written Contract: Required for any job over $500
  • Deposit Limits: Cannot exceed 1/3 of total cost or $1,000 (whichever is less)
  • 3-Day Right to Cancel: Cooling-off period for contracts signed at your home
  • Warranty Information: Must be provided in writing
If Problems Arise: Document everything (photos, emails, texts), communicate in writing, file complaint with NJ Division of Consumer Affairs at 1-800-242-5846.
