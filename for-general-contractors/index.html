General Contractor Info

Licensed New Jersey Home Improvement Contractor · HIC #13VH10808800 · COI and W-9 available upon request with bid packages.

  • Service Area Atlantic, Ocean, and Cape May Counties. Larger scopes may be considered outside the area based on schedule and logistics.
  • Typical Scopes Showers and wet areas, floor tile, backsplashes, large-format installs, punch and takeover corrections (by scope).
  • Documentation Written scope, change orders when needed, progress photos, and closeout notes delivered digitally.

Project Types & Capabilities

Bathrooms & Wet Areas

Tile assemblies planned and installed as systems: substrate readiness, waterproofing continuity (when applicable), detailing at penetrations, and layout that lands clean. Coordination with plumbing/electrical trades by GC.

Spec Homes & Repeating Plans

Repeatable scopes for consistent floor plans. Clear allowances, predictable sequences, and documented closeout for smoother turnover.

Small Commercial / Occupied Spaces

Restrooms, lobbies, and small retail tile scopes with attention to protection, safety, and clean daily shutdowns for occupied sites.

Punchlist & Takeover Work

Detail corrections, finish repairs, and takeover completion where sites need clean closeout. Scope is documented up front to avoid drift.

Bidding, Scheduling & Change Orders

  • Estimates: Send plans/takeoffs or schedule a walk. Proposals include written scope, assumptions, and material allowances where needed.
  • Scheduling: Dates are confirmed in writing when site readiness is verified (substrate condition, waterproofing strategy, trade access, and materials on site).
  • Change orders: If scope changes, pricing is documented and approved before additional work proceeds.
  • Handoffs: Brief updates at key stages (pre-tile readiness, waterproofing complete, setting complete, ready for next trade).
  • Closeout: Photo log + punch completion notes for straightforward turnover.

Owner-operated means bandwidth is real. If the schedule is tight, we’ll say it early and propose realistic sequencing.

What You Can Expect on Every Scope

  • System-based planning for wet areas (membrane + drain integration when applicable), with verification steps identified in scope.
  • Respect for other trades: protected finishes, clear staging, and clean handoffs.
  • Clean, safe work areas with reasonable dust control and end-of-day cleanup appropriate to the scope.
  • One point of contact: Devon is the on-site lead from layout through punch.
  • Documentation delivered digitally: scope, COI/W-9 upon request, change orders when needed, and photo logs.

Want a tile sub who documents the work and keeps scope clean? Send plans or request availability.

Insurance & Documentation

General liability insurance and standard bid documents can be provided upon request. If your project requires onboarding, badges, or jobsite access procedures, we’ll handle it as part of mobilization planning.
